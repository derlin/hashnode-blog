***Key takeaways:***

## How I fell in love with programming

I discovered programming quite late. I was more of a bookworm in my teens and loathed technology. We had a computer at home, but I never really used it. I just remember watching my sister chat on caramail, and dictating my handwritten reports to my mum so she could turn them into Word documents.

After graduating college, I started university in Human Sciences (Sociology, Communication, and a minor in Philosophy), which required only basic knowledge in Google and Word, sometimes SPSS (for statistics). My roommate also showed me how to watch animes (using MegaUpload), but that was pretty much it.

As Human Sciences didn't open a lot of opportunities, I planned a Master's in Information Technologies. I didn't really understand what it meant, just that it would lead to job opportunities. I had to take some extra credits first, and since I had time to spare, I went as a free auditor to random classes. I stumbled upon one called "*Programming in Java*". Not understanding half of the title, I gathered it would be fun. This was a revelation.

The class was based solely on [**Java notes**](https://math.hws.edu/javanotes/) (version 5 at the time) and after the first week, I printed the whole book/site, assembled it in a blinder, and started going through it every night, one exercise after the other. It took around 2 months.

I slowly realized that computers weren't my enemies, here on earth only to make me miserable. Instead, I discovered a world of logic, order, determinism (well, this is not true, but when you start it can feel like it), creativity, and most of all beauty. I was suddenly able to create things, to control technology, this was awesome.

I dropped my Master's and went to the opposite side of the road, where stood an Engineering School:

> *Hi, my name is Lucy, I know nothing but I am really motivated.*

They accepted me despite my lack of experience (I was a girl, and finished my first Bachelor's as the valedictorian, which helped), and I thus started anew, at a Bachelor's level. This was in 2011.

## My first project

After finishing [Java notes](https://math.hws.edu/javanotes/) (version 5), I wanted to create a full "app".

The result is available on GitHub: [https://github.com/derlin/my-first-project](https://github.com/derlin/my-first-project). It is a completely useless Java app, which is yet oddly satisfying. I was so proud at the time (and still am, in a way)! I even compiled special messages that I sent by email to my loved ones (at the time, they were likely to click on a jar from an attachment).

I didn't know about IDEs, build scripts, etc., and compiled manually back then (yep, `javac` and all). I am glad I started this way, learning the ropes before switching to all-encompassing tools that hide all the complexities behind a shiny UI.

## Why share all this?

For once, I wanted to talk about what made me fall in love with programming. Knowing why you do what you do is important, and passion is what will make you last in this industry.

I also wanted to explain a bit about my journey, hoping this may inspire people - especially women - who hesitate on changing their course. I started from scratch at 23, and am doing fine. You could do it as well.

Finally, I thought my first app was cool, so why not advertise it?
