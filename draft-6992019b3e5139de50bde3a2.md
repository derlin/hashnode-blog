Here is a complete tutorial on how to create book-folding art using a simple technique called “line templates”. Line templates can be created easily using my free online tool: [bookfold.derlin.ch](https://bookfold.derlin.ch).

This tutorial is meant to exemplify what you can do with line templates, but this is just an example. Everything I present in TODO\_OTHER\_TUTORIAL can be achieved with this technique!

## Requirements

## Find a good image

### Source Image

First, save the image above and load it into [bookfold.derlin.ch](http://bookfold.derlin.ch) using the “source” on the left. The source image will be trimmed and converted into black and white, and this transformed image will be the source from which the pattern is created. The white will be the “background”, the black will be the design.

You have different controls to play with the source image:

*   **Resize Image**: If your image is too big or too small, you can adjust its size directly (in percent). Resizing will impact the pattern and the number of pages required.
    
*   **Black/White Threshold**: The black and white conversion of the image is based on a simple algorithm. For each pixel, the tool averages the values of the three RGB channels and considers the pixel black if the average is above a threshold, white otherwise. Changing the threshold (default to 128) will change what is considered black, useful for grayscale or colored images.
    
*   **Transparent is White**: If your image has transparent areas, you can decide whether the transparency is treated as white (default) or black.
    
*   **Inverse colors**: you can also decide to flip the black and the white.
    

### Pattern Generation

The pattern generation controls change the line template. To create the template, the tool scans the transformed black and white image (the source) horizontally and takes samples every X pixels. This X is determined with the “Line spacing” option.

*   **Line spacing**: The spacing controls how the tool scans the image. The higher the line spacing, the fewer details (and pages) will be rendered. A line spacing of 1 is equivalent to the original image.
    

The rest of the controls are visual only - they do not impact the number of pages or the final result - but are useful to make the pattern easy to work with after printing.

*   **Line Thickness**: This controls the width of the lines in the pattern. I usually use a thickness of 2 pixels to ensure the segments are fully visible after printing the pattern.
    
*   **Horizontal Stretch**: This stretches the pattern horizontally without changing the image itself. If your segments are too close together, it will be harder to follow and work with (especially if you don’t have a good printer). 1 means no stretch at all (same as original).
    
*   **Padding**: you can add padding around the pattern, again only for visual ease.
    

### Number of pages

At the top of the pattern, you have different numbers. They convey the same information, depending on whether you do a Cut & Fold or a Traditional Fold (MMF).

*   The number of **lines** tells you how many vertical lines exist, or how many *physical* pages (actual leaflets) will be necessary for a Cut & Fold.
    
*   The number of **segments** tells you how many separate black segments are present, or how many physical pages will be necessary for a traditional fold (MMF).
    
*   The number of **pages** tells you how many book pages will be necesary. Every physical pages has two sides, and thus makes up for two numbered pages in a book. It is thus easier to directly think with page numbers, which are the number of lines (or segments) multiplied by two.
    

### Export

Once you have a line template that looks good for your project, you can export it. The easiest is to right-click > “copy image”, but you can also download it to a PNG.

The **Height** control lets you choose the height, in centimeter or pixels, of the image. When choosing centimeters, ensure the DPI you choose matches the one of your printer.

For this tutorial, I resized the initial image to 90%, and used a line spacing of 4, which will require **322 pages**. I also set the horizontal stretch to 1.5 and line thickness of 2 of convenience.

## Select a book

Now, you will need to print the pattern according to the book you want to fold.

The most important criterion to choose a book is the number of pages. As [bookfold.derlin.ch](https://bookfold.derlin.ch) told you, this pattern requires 102 physical pages, or 204 page numbers. Thus, look for a book that has at least 210 pages available (keep extra for potential mistakes). And be careful, most books do not start at page 1!

Here are some more tips on choosing a book:

*   needless to say, a hardback book is what you are looking for.
    
*   Avoid books with colored or glassy pages (or remove them before you start)
    
*   the thickness of the pages impacts the final result. Too thick, and the result will feel very sturdy, be too heavy to stand on its own, and make the opening very wide. Too thin, and the paper will break easily.
    

Once you found a book to work with, pick a page and measure its height.

## Print the pattern

The pattern needs to be printed at the right size, and at the right offset.

For example, let’s say your book pages have a height of 20 cm. You want to have some margins on the top and bottom of the design, so 15 cm can be a good fit. You also want your design centered, so the offset of the template from the top of the printed page should be (20-15)/2 = 2.5 cm.

So you need to print the template on a landscape 4A paper, with an height of 15 cm and an offset from the top at 2.5 cm. There are multiple ways to achieve this, but here is what I use:

*   Copy and paste the template from [bookfold.derlin.ch](https://bookfold.derlin.ch) into a Google Docs ([https://docs.new](https://docs.new))
    
*   Click on the image, and open the “size and position” menu:
    
    *   Under “size and rotation”, set the height to 15 cm
        
    *   Under “position”, ensure you select “fix position on page” and set the y offset to 2.5 cm
        

You can now print the template on an A4 page.

![](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1771267751962/0d5da2ad-c9d0-48b2-9bb8-2410018c8ffe.png align="center")

## Compute the start and end pages

You want to have as many unfolded pages on the left and on the right of the design.

1.  Take the last page number (say 365) minus the first page number (say 5): 357 - 5 = 352
    
2.  Out of those 352, 322 will be used for the design: 352 - 322 = 30
    
3.  We want the same number of pages at the start and finish: 30 / 2 = 15
    
4.  The page numbers are shifted by 5 (since the first page number is 5), so the design should start at page 20 and end at page 20 + 322 = 342.
    

I usually shift by 1 or 2 to account for mistakes. The first page to fold would thus be the one numbered 19.

## Let's fold

### Mark the pages

![](https://cloudmate-test.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/uploads/covers/639ace6b0517875138ea752f/63a667ab-d7be-4eb7-b4b0-b68c247f140e.png align="center")

Place the pattern behind the first page and move it until it perfectly aligns with the top of the book and the first line of the pattern aligns with the side of the page. Draw small marks at the beginning and end of every black segment. Once done, draw a dot at the top of the line on the pattern, so you know where you are.

![](https://cloudmate-test.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/uploads/covers/639ace6b0517875138ea752f/d9eca768-c00f-4006-8bbd-f83267c44e23.png align="center")

Head over to the second page and repeat. Do this for the first 10 pages. I usually start with batches of 10 (from one red line to the next), and increase it to 20 at a time once I feel comfortable.

### Fold the first and the last mark

Go back to the first page and do a complete fold on the first and the last mark. The first page of the pattern only has two marks (first and last), but for other pages, you will have marks in the middle: those will be cut later.

![](https://cloudmate-test.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/uploads/covers/639ace6b0517875138ea752f/829427bf-620b-4d18-ad92-9d391cdae890.png align="center")

I usually fold all the way to the binding, but some people prefer leaving a 1-2 cm at the inner of the book. This is up to you.

### Draw a helper line before cutting

Draw a line at about 1 to 2 cm into the page. This line will help you know how deep to cut and make the folds more regular.

![](https://cloudmate-test.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/uploads/covers/639ace6b0517875138ea752f/185d2243-227f-4816-9c75-714178c86b32.png align="center")

I personally have a ruler that is exactly 1.5 cm wide, which is very helpful: I align it with the side of the page, making this step faster.

### Cut and fold the other marks

![](https://cloudmate-test.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/uploads/covers/639ace6b0517875138ea752f/92c23716-abe5-4648-8df0-9cb597009fbd.png align="center")

Cut the page horizontally from the mark to the helper line drawn before. Make it as straight as possible (use the text to guide you). Then, fold one out of two. The extremes are always unfolded!
